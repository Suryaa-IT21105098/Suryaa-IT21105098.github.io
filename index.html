Mitigating Cyber Supply Chain Risks in Finance

Literature Review

The literature indicates that financial institutions increasingly rely on third-party vendors, intensifying the need for robust cybersecurity frameworks. Traditional risk assessment methods have proven inadequate in addressing modern cybersecurity challenges, as they often fail to adapt dynamically to evolving threats. Integrating machine learning and zero-trust architectures has shown potential for improving data classification, sensitivity analysis, and secure data sharing. However, a comprehensive solution combining these technologies for real-time cybersecurity risk management within financial supply chains remains underexplored​.

Research Problem

Financial institutions face significant cybersecurity risks in their supply chains, particularly due to third-party vendors with access to critical data and systems. Current risk assessment frameworks are limited in their ability to identify and mitigate these evolving cyber threats. There is a need for an integrated, scalable solution that can streamline vendor assessments, classify sensitive data, and provide tailored threat intelligence to enhance the security posture within the financial sector​.

“

This research proposes a multi-component framework that includes a centralized risk assessment gateway, sensitive data classification, and a machine learning-driven threat intelligence platform. By combining a zero-trust policy with machine learning, this approach enhances security measures, offering financial institutions a unified model for identifying and managing cybersecurity risks associated with third-party vendors​.

Sub Objective 2

Sub Objective 3

04

Sub Objective 4

Integrate zero-trust principles and strategies into the risk management process and make it a more secure, transparent, and resilient ecosystem

Methodology

The proposed methodology involves developing a secure, web-based risk assessment portal where vendors can submit information for evaluation. Using a combination of machine learning algorithms and natural language processing, the system will classify text and image data for sensitivity, while zero-trust protocols enforce access restrictions. A threat intelligence platform will analyze external threat feeds, providing security teams with actionable insights for proactive risk mitigation​
